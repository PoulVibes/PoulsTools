# World of Warcraft Core Systems & Fundamentals

## 1. Character Classes
Every character belongs to a specific class that dictates their abilities and potential roles.
*   **Warrior**: Master of melee weapons and plate armor. Can be a sturdy Tank or a powerful Melee DPS.
*   **Paladin**: Holy warriors who use the Light to heal, tank, or deal melee damage.
*   **Hunter**: Masters of the wild who use ranged weapons and tame beasts as pets.
*   **Rogue**: Mercenaries using stealth, poisons, and dual-wielding to strike from the shadows.
*   **Priest**: Spiritual leaders who excel at healing or using shadowy magic for ranged damage.
*   **Mage**: Masters of the arcane, fire, and frost who deal high ranged magical damage.
*   **Warlock**: Summoners of demons who use dark magic and damage-over-time effects.
*   **Shaman**: Elemental masters who use totems to heal or deal ranged and melee damage.
*   **Druid**: Versatile shapeshifters capable of performing all roles: Tank, Healer, and both Ranged and Melee DPS.
*   **Death Knight, Monk, Demon Hunter, Evoker**: Additional classes introduced in various expansions with unique mechanics like runes, chi, or fel magic.

## 2. Combat Roles
In group play, players fulfill one of three primary "jobs":
*   **Tank**: High-health characters who draw enemy "aggro" (threat) and absorb damage to protect the group.
*   **Healer**: Responsible for restoring the health of allies and providing protective buffs.
*   **Damage Dealer (DPS)**: Focused on dealing the maximum amount of damage to enemies. Further divided into **Melee** (close range) and **Ranged** (distance).

## 3. Specializations ("Specs")
At level 10, characters choose a specialization that defines their specific playstyle and role within their class.
*   **Pure Classes**: Classes like Mages or Rogues have three specializations that are all DPS, but with different mechanics (e.g., Fire vs. Frost).
*   **Hybrid Classes**: Classes like Druids or Paladins can swap between specs to fulfill completely different roles (e.g., healing as Holy vs. tanking as Protection).

## 4. Content Types
The game is divided between the leveling journey and "Endgame" activities:
*   **Quests**: Traditional tasks given by NPCs to progress the story and gain experience.
*   **Dungeons**: Instanced challenges for 5 players, ending in boss encounters. Difficulties include Normal, Heroic, and Mythic.
*   **Raids**: Large-scale instanced encounters for groups of 10–30 players, requiring high coordination.
*   **Player vs. Player (PvP)**: Combat against other players in **Battlegrounds** (objective-based maps) or **Arenas** (competitive team deathmatch).
*   **World Quests & Delves**: Solo-friendly or small group endgame activities that take place in the open world or mini-scenarios.

## 5. Leveling Systems
*   **Experience (XP)**: Gained by killing enemies, completing quests, or exploring the world.
*   **Chromie Time**: A system allowing players to choose which past expansion's story they want to use for leveling from 10–60.
*   **Level Cap**: The maximum level reachable in the current expansion (currently 80 in *The War Within*).
*   **Talents**: Points earned while leveling that are spent in a tree-based system to customize abilities.
